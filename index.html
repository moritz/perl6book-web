Perl 6 Fundamenatls

Moritz Lenz, initiator of the Perl 6 documentation project, is writing a book about Perl 6.

The Book

"Perl 6 Fundamentals" introduces Perl 6 using practical, short examples, and explains the language features from those examples along the way.

It features discussions on how to test the code, refactoring to make it more idiomatic, and doesn't shy away from concurrency and keeping persistent state.

Status

The is being published by Apress. They have received the final manuscript, and are now working on publishing it.

Other Perl 6 Books

  • Perl 6 at a Glance by Andrew Shitov is available in print form. Migrating to Perl 6 by the same author is due in May 2017.
  • Ken Youens-Clark's free ebook on metagenomics spends more than half of its volume on explaining Perl 6.
  • Think Perl 6 How to Think Like a Computer Scientist by Laurent Rosenfeld is an introduction to programming using Perl 6 for its examples. It's available for preorder both in electronic and print format.
  • Learning Perl 6 by brian d foy is a work in progress, to be published by O'Reilly Media.
  • Web Application Development in Perl 6 by Gabor Szabo is a work in progress.
